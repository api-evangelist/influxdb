Telegraf is incredibly good at what it does: collecting metrics, logs, and events from just about anywhere and sending them wherever you need. But once Telegraf becomes part of your production telemetry pipeline, spread across environments, teams, regions, and edge locations, the hard part isn’t installing agents; it’s operating them. Configs drift. “Temporary” overrides linger. Rolling out changes across hundreds (or thousands) of agents becomes a careful, manual process. And when something breaks, the first question is rarely about the data—it’s about the fleet: which configuration is running where, and is every agent healthy? That’s the problem Telegraf Enterprise is built to solve. Today, we’re opening the Telegraf Enterprise beta to the broader Telegraf community so you can help us validate the product where it matters most: at scale. What is Telegraf Enterprise? Telegraf Enterprise is a commercial offering for organizations running Telegraf at scale and needing centralized management, governance, and support. It brings together two key components: Telegraf Controller : A control plane (UI + API) that centralizes Telegraf configuration management and fleet health visibility. Telegraf Enterprise Support : Official support for Telegraf Controller and official Telegraf plugins, designed for teams that need dependable response times and expert guidance. It’s built for real-world, large-scale agent deployments, where Telegraf isn’t a tool you occasionally touch, but a platform you rely on. Meet Telegraf Controller: your Telegraf control plane At the heart of Telegraf Enterprise is Telegraf Controller , which centralizes two things teams struggle with most at scale: Configuration Management That Doesn’t Collapse Under Growth Telegraf Controller helps you create and manage configurations to support consistency across environments while still allowing necessary variation. Core capabilities include: Centralized configuration creation and editing Templates and parameterization to reuse configs safely Label-based organization (so fleets don’t devolve into a long list of “agent-123”) Bulk operations for fleet-wide changes Environment variable and parameter management Plugin metadata visibility to simplify config authoring and maintenance Fleet-Wide Health Visibility Telegraf Controller gives you a single view into the overall status of your agent deployments, so you can understand: Which agents are reporting as expected Where health issues are clustering What changed recently, and what might be correlated In other words, you don’t just manage Telegraf. You operate it. Designed to fit your telemetry stack Telegraf Enterprise is designed to work with the way teams actually deploy Telegraf. It does not require InfluxDB . You can use the Telegraf Controller regardless of where your telemetry data is going. Configuration delivery follows a pull-based model , where agents fetch configuration over HTTP. This keeps change management predictable and compatible with locked-down environments. It’s built to support hundreds to thousands of agents , with production-grade storage options and a modern UI + API architecture for automation. Why we’re running this beta This beta is open to any Telegraf user who wants to test-drive Telegraf Controller. The focus of the beta is simple: Test Telegraf Controller at scale : We want to validate how well Telegraf Controller holds up when you connect real fleets—hundreds or thousands of agents—with real operational behaviors. Gather feedback from the community: We’re intentionally inviting community input early, while we’re still shaping the GA experience. What workflows are missing? What’s confusing? What would make this tool indispensable in your environment? At this stage, your feedback directly influences what Telegraf Enterprise becomes. Enterprise support that matches production expectations Operating telemetry pipelines is a production responsibility, and when Telegraf is part of that pipeline, you need support that understands the stakes. Telegraf Enterprise includes support designed for teams that need: Clear expectations for response and escalation Coverage for Telegraf Controller and official Telegraf plugins Help diagnosing issues and reducing operational risk as fleets grow This is especially valuable when Telegraf is deployed across multiple teams, environments, or customer sites, where operational consistency matters as much as collection capability. Who is Telegraf Enterprise for? Telegraf Enterprise is built for organizations that manage Telegraf fleets at a meaningful scale, including: Platform engineering and SRE teams DevOps organizations operating across multi-cloud / hybrid / edge Managed service providers delivering telemetry as a service Compliance-sensitive teams that need standardized configurations and governance If you’re running a small number of agents and are comfortable managing configs manually, you may not need Telegraf Enterprise today. But if Telegraf is everywhere—and your team is responsible for keeping it reliable—centralized control quickly becomes less “nice to have” and more “how did we operate without this?” Packaging: free and enterprise options Telegraf Controller A free tier is available for teams that want centralized configuration management and visibility with pre-defined limits. Telegraf Enterprise For teams operating Telegraf as critical infrastructure, Telegraf Enterprise includes the Telegraf Controller packaged with enterprise support. The key difference : the Telegraf Enterprise is built for scale and operational reliability, with support and capabilities aligned to production fleet management. Getting started with Telegraf Controller Telegraf Enterprise is designed for teams operating Telegraf as a core part of production telemetry pipelines. If Telegraf is already how you collect metrics, logs, and events across your infrastructure, Telegraf Controller is the missing piece that helps you operate that collection layer like a platform—not a pile of configs.
